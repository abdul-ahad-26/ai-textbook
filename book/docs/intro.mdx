# Introduction to Physical AI

> **Focus & Theme:** AI Systems in the Physical World. Embodied Intelligence.
>
> **Goal:** Bridge the gap between the *digital brain* and the *physical body*. You will apply your AI knowledge to control humanoid robots in simulated and real-world environments.

The future of work will be a partnership between people, intelligent agents (AI software), and
robots. For the last decade, the most capable AI systems lived entirely in the digital world —
they read text, generated images, and answered questions, but they could not *act* in the room
you are sitting in. **Physical AI** is the discipline that changes this: it builds AI systems that
function in reality and comprehend physical laws.

This textbook teaches you to **design, simulate, and deploy humanoid robots** capable of natural
human interaction, using the modern open-source and industrial stack: **ROS 2**, **Gazebo**,
**Unity**, **NVIDIA Isaac**, and **Vision-Language-Action (VLA)** models.

## Why "embodied" intelligence?

A language model predicting the next token has no body, no sensors, and no consequences. An
*embodied* agent is different in three fundamental ways:

1. **It is situated.** It exists at a specific place and time, perceiving the world through sensors
   (cameras, LiDAR, IMUs, force/torque) and changing the world through actuators (motors, grippers).
2. **It is subject to physics.** Gravity, friction, inertia, latency, and noise are not abstractions —
   they determine whether the robot stays upright or falls over.
3. **It closes the loop.** Perception → cognition → action → perception. The agent's own actions change
   what it will perceive next, which is the defining feature of *control*.

Humanoid robots are poised to excel in our human-centered world because they share our physical form
and can be trained with abundant data from interacting in human environments. This is a significant
transition from AI models confined to digital environments to **embodied intelligence that operates
in physical space**.

## The architecture you will build

Throughout this course, every concept maps onto a concrete lab architecture:

| Layer | Hardware / Software | Function |
|-------|---------------------|----------|
| **Sim Rig** | RTX workstation + Ubuntu 22.04 | Runs Isaac Sim, Gazebo, Unity; trains LLM/VLA models |
| **Edge Brain** | NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano | Runs the inference stack; where your code is deployed |
| **Sensors** | RealSense camera + LiDAR | Feed real-world data to the AI |
| **Actuator** | Unitree Go2 / G1 (shared) | Receives motor commands from the Jetson |

You do not need to own this hardware to learn. The entire course is designed to run in **simulation
first** — you will prove your robot works in Gazebo and Isaac Sim before a single motor turns.

## The four modules

This book follows the course's four-module arc, from the robot's "nervous system" up to its "mind":

- **Module 1 — The Robotic Nervous System (ROS 2):** the middleware that lets software talk to motors and sensors.
- **Module 2 — The Digital Twin (Gazebo & Unity):** physics simulation and high-fidelity environments.
- **Module 3 — The AI-Robot Brain (NVIDIA Isaac):** photorealistic simulation, VSLAM, and navigation.
- **Module 4 — Vision-Language-Action (VLA):** the convergence of LLMs and robotics, ending in a capstone autonomous humanoid.

## Learning outcomes

By the end of this textbook you will be able to:

1. Explain Physical AI principles and embodied intelligence.
2. Use **ROS 2** (nodes, topics, services, actions) for robotic control.
3. Simulate robots with **Gazebo** and visualize them in **Unity**.
4. Develop perception and navigation with the **NVIDIA Isaac** platform.
5. Design humanoid robots for natural interactions.
6. Integrate large language models for **conversational, voice-driven robotics**.
